01 The problem

Where blockchain projects go wrong.

Most failed Web3 projects don't fail on vision — they fail on security, integration and usability.

1

Insecure contracts

Unaudited smart contracts holding real value, one exploit away from irreversible loss.

2

Disconnected from reality

On-chain logic with no clean bridge to the off-chain systems, data and payments a business actually uses.

3

Unusable for normal users

Experiences that assume everyone understands wallets, gas and seed phrases — so adoption stalls.

4

Gas & scalability surprises

Designs that work in a demo but become slow or expensive at real transaction volumes.

08 FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Do we actually need blockchain for this?

Often the honest answer is no — and we'll tell you. Blockchain earns its place when you need decentralised trust, verifiable ownership or tamper-evident records. If a database is the right tool, we'll recommend that instead.

How do you handle smart contract security?

Security drives the whole process: established patterns, exhaustive automated and manual testing, fuzzing, threat modelling and coordination of external audits before mainnet deployment.

Which chains do you build on?

Primarily Ethereum and EVM-compatible L2s like Polygon and Base, chosen for cost, security and ecosystem. We advise based on your transaction volume, cost sensitivity and user base.

Can blockchain integrate with our existing systems?

Yes — we specialise in bridging on-chain logic to off-chain back-ends, data and payments, so blockchain becomes part of your stack rather than a disconnected experiment.

How much does a blockchain project cost?

It varies widely with complexity and audit requirements, typically from €20,000 for a focused contract-and-dApp build upward. Audits are scoped separately. Discovery produces a clear, fixed proposal.

Exploring blockchain — or unsure if you should?

Tell us the problem you're trying to solve. We'll give you a straight answer on whether on-chain is the right tool.
